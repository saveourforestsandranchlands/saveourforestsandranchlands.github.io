Appeal for Funds to Challenge "Failed Planning" of "Derelict Board"!

May 28, 2002

Dear Harriet,

The San Diego Union-Tribune editorial "Derelict Board" could not have said it better when they wrote, "the county keeps trying to develop farmland." That is what Save Our Forest And Ranchlands and our supporters have been saying all along about GPA 01-01 (formerly known as GPA 96-03). That is also why for seven years we have prevented the County from destroying 200,000 acres of farm and rangelands.

We must now continue in our fight to save San Diego's backcountry against a "Derelict Board" of Supervisors. So we are once again appealing for your help to raise funds to carry on SOFAR's work.

In the past, your support has given us the strength to be victorious in this monumental struggle over the fate of the remaining rangelands and wilderness of San Diego's mountain areas. With each victory we have gained ground in both the political and environmental arena, while the Board of Supervisors keep losing credibility as stewards of our land resources.

In the battle over GPA 01-01, we accomplished three fundamental changes:

  • A grading ordinance that requires environmental review before any clearing of the land
  • A minimum lot size increase on affected lands
  • Admission by the County that the real nature of this project is residential use, not farming
    At the beginning of GPA 01-01 six years ago, the County claimed that 100% of the land would be used for farming, and now after losing three times in court the County is finally admitting that less than 3% of the land will actually be used for agriculture.

Throughout this battle, SOFAR's litigation has been based on the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), and we have ridden this horse as far as it can possibly take us. Because CEQA is primarily an informational procedure and cannot force the county to make the fundamental policy changes needed to protect our backcountry, our attorneys advised SOFAR to settle this case. However, in another case SOFAR remains the court appointed land-use authority on 50,000 acres of land in the Cleveland National Forest and as author of the Forest Conservation Initiative stands guard over forest resources.

It is now time for SOFAR to move on to different battlefields to challenge our County's ever expanding sprawl policies.

We have retained the law firm of Shute, Mihaly, and Weinberger to comment on the massive growth inducing impacts of the Imperial County water transfer project. It is SOFAR's position that we must have a plan for the sustainable use of our existing resources before we import additional resources that would only be wasted by the current pattern of sprawl development.

And as you know, the County of San Diego is currently updating its twenty-year-old General Plan (2020 Plan). Significant changes in our land use laws are being proposed. These changes will either chart the way to a healthy sustainable future or they will fatally disable our last safeguards against a relentless tide of sprawl development and traffic gridlock.

Even while the County is moving forward with a so-called 2020 "smart growth" plan, it continues to process hundreds of residential and commercial projects. SOFAR is on guard. We are currently monitoring 90 major projects covering 30,000 acres. The growing list of outrageous projects includes the development of a 500-acre economically viable avocado grove in Lakeside for the construction of 200 unaffordable homes. We continue to successfully oppose the East County Ralph's and Albertson's mega-stores, while Von's has recently decided to jump into the ring with its version of an Alpine strip-mall. Everybody is gambling on sprawl!

The 2020 planning process is at a critical stage and the County's "good faith" clearly cannot be relied upon. Therefore, we are now asking you to join us in our fight to preserve not simply the health and integrity of the wild open space in the backcountry, but their very existence.


  • We must build on the momentum of our previous successful challenges and create public recognition of what is at stake
  • We must prevent further loss of our agricultural and wildland resources
  • We must insist the county adopt a plan that protects the vital natural resources and rural character that makes San Diego's backcountry a national treasure
  • We must do all we can to urge the county to adopt well-defined urban growth boundaries
  • The only way SOFAR will be able to accomplish these monumental goals is with the help of supporters like you. Please consider making a contribution to SOFAR today, so that we can continue to lead the charge to save San Diego's backcountry.

Thank you for your past support and thank you for your contribution now at this critical moment of need.

Sincerely,
Duncan McFetridge; President
