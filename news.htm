  • CURRENT STATUS OF PROPOSED PROJECTS IN THE BACKCOUNTRY:

    ALPINE ALBERTSON'S AND LAKESIDE RALPH'S UPDATE

    In the year 2000, SOFAR teamed up in two coalitions to oppose two chain- market retailers with plans to build large grocery complexes along rural portions of the I-8 corridor. We campaigned vigorously and were able to successfully make fair arguments that the impacts from these projects should require a full Environmental Review (EIR). SOFAR considers these cases to be major victories in the battle against sprawl in San Diego's backcountry. In both cases, due to the firm resolve and persistent efforts of these coalitions, the proponents voluntarily decided to do full environmental analyses. We expect draft EIRs to be out late summer for both the Albertson's and Ralph's projects when, once again, we intend to be prepared to challenge these projects which threaten to degrade the character of our rural towns.
    To help us battle these giants, and other projects like them, please make a contribution to our campaign against the Gamble on Sprawl.

    SOFAR is monitoring these projects, as well as ALL other backcountry projects, closely.
