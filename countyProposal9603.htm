Save our Forest and Ranchlands GPA 96-03 Alternative

Save Our Forest And Ranchlands (SOFAR) requests your group's support for SOFAR's recommendation for General Plan Amendment (GPA) 96-03:

1. The County of San Diego adopt an Agriculture Element to our General Plan to cover several hundred thousand acres of irrigated cropland west of the County Water Authority (CWA) infrastructure line.

2. Adopt lot size minimums for Agriculture Preserve (Ag 20) farmlands and open space of 80 acres east of the CWA infrastructure line, 40 acres west of the CWA line, and 10 acres for prime farmland west of the CWA line.

3. Environmental review to take place for clearing or subdivision of land.

BACKGROUND:


The County’s project, GPA 96-03, is the response to SOFAR’s lawsuit against the County for not adopting an Agriculture Element and not properly zoning our Agricultural Preserve lands. The Superior Court ruled that the General Plan was missing a mandatory Agriculture Element and the urban zoning of 8-acre minimum lot size set by the County for Agricultural Preserve (Ag 20) lands was illegal. All subdivision activity and issuance of building permits was stopped.


GPA 96-03, as proposed by the County, allows 10-acre minimum lot sizes for Ag 20 lands west of the CWA infrastructure line. Ag 20 lands east of the CWA infrastructure line would have a 40-acre minimum lot size or a 10-acre minimum lot size if it can be demonstrated that there is evidence of adequate soils, topography, and water availability to support the proposed agriculture.


The County’s own Environmental Impact Report (EIR) for GPA 96-03 assumes complete clearing of the 200,000 acres of Agricultural Preserve (Ag 20) farmlands. This would have tremendous negative ramifications on San Diego County’s watersheds, especially those that fill our largest reservoirs: El Capitan, San Vicente, and Lake Hodges.


Unmitigated significant impacts due to GPA 96-03 are:

Transportation/Circulation – The project could significantly impact approximately 39 road miles.

Biological Resources – Some sensitive biological resources could be significantly impacted by the project.


Water Resources – Could deplete groundwater in areas that are groundwater limited and result in significant water quality impacts from fertilizer application.


Visual Quality/Aesthetics – Change in landform alteration from grading and clearing could result in significant visual quality impacts.


Cultural/Paleontological – Significant impacts could occur due to grading and clearing.


Noise – Future non-discretionary uses and traffic could cause significant noise impacts.


Air Quality - Could result in increased inhalable particulate matter.


Geological Resources – Grading and landform modification could increase the potential for impacts related to flooding, erosion, siltation, and unstable soils.


This assumption of complete clearing of the backcountry is not done to facilitate agriculture; this is a masquerade for the complete development of San Diego’s backcountry. The lot size minimums proposed by the County will not support SOFAR recommends a more realistic alternative GPA 96-03 that would meet the requirements of the Williamson Act, would promote sustainable agriculture, would meet the project’s objectives, and that would protect Ag 20 lands from inappropriate development. This alternative would designate all Ag 20 lands west of the CWA infrastructure line at a 40-acre minimum lot size. This is the minimum size needed to enter a Williamson Act contract. Any Ag 20 land west of the CWA line that is prime farmland as defined under the Act, would be 10-acre minimum lot size. This is the minimum size for prime farmland for a Williamson Act contract. All Ag 20 lands east of the CWA infrastructure line would be 80-acre minimum lot size. This is the minimum size for grazing lands to enter into a Williamson Act contract. By the County’s own evaluation, these backcountry ranchlands, dependent on limited groundwater, are suited for grazing.


Seven conservation groups have endorsed the SOFAR Alternative to GPA 96-03: the Sierra Club, Buena Vista & Palomar Audubon Societies, Backcountry Coalition, Preserve Wild Santee, Save South Bay, Volcan Mountain Preserve Foundation, and Friends of Julian. The Ramona, Crest/Dehesa, and Boulevard Planning/Sponsor Groups have endorsed our alternative. Please join us in recommending to the County that they adopt SOFAR’s Alternative to GPA 96-03.
