GPA 96-03 -- The Aftermath, The Truth

On Wednesday April 5th, the County Board of Supervisors unanimously approved the General Plan Amendment 96-03 (GPA 96-03) that regulates land use on approximately 200,000 acres of wildlands in north and east San Diego County.

SOFAR is firm in its position that small lot sizes encourages sprawl development and NOT farming as claimed by County staff and the local Farm Bureau (Building Industry front-men).

In addition, we believe that GPA 96-03 is an illegal plan that is based on a flawed Environmental Impact Report (EIR) and on a calculated attempt by County officials to deliberately mislead Federal and State agencies and the public.

First of all, the EIR amazingly concluded that for the county-wide unmitigable impacts found in virtually every CEQA category there were no feasible mitigation measures possible and, equally amazingly, that no further environmental review would be required for agriculturally clearing at the parcel level. Thus, the Board had to sign overriding considerations for each unmitigable impact category. But the County failed to investigate, as required by law, alternatives that would mitigate of their proposed project. No consideration was given to an alternative that would preserve existing uses. At the hearing, SOFAR introduced a study by rangeland experts which demonstrates that the San Diego backcountry in its current use is supported by viable, productive rangelands. SOFAR argued that the County should continue the hearing to consider the rangeland alternative that was not considered in the EIR that would obviously mitigate the impacts of the radical change in use proposed by the County's project. The County's own agricultural expert who opined that cattle grazing was no longer viable in the backcountry admitted at the Board hearing that he was not qualified to address rangeland issues.

Of equal concern to SOFAR is the deliberate misrepresentation of the issue of densification. In late March, the Environmental Protection Agency, after having reviewed the EIR for GPA 96-03, wrote a letter to the County expressing concern over the impacts of the project to water quality and to the future water supply to the San Diego region. These detailed statements from a federal agency indicated there was serious trouble with the County's plan. However, the EPA revised their letter just prior to the hearing (literally, hours before) after County staff convinced them that GPA 96-03 would not result in an increase but a decrease in density.

This argument is not based on fact or the law.

County officials took advantage of abstruse technical terminology in their communications with the EPA, knowing full well that to compare the current proposal with what the Courts ruled to be totally illegal (8-acre zoning), was what CEQA calls a "Plan to Plan Comparison" which is expressly PROHIBITED BY LAW in evaluating the impacts of a proposed project. The Board took advantage of the EPA's retraction and all but dismissed any discussion on the relevance of water quality impacts.

The matter is not over. The County's proposal and the Board's decision must be deemed sufficient by the Court.

SOFAR and its attorneys are reviewing carefully the record in order to defend the public good in a matter so consequential to the quality of life for generations to come.
