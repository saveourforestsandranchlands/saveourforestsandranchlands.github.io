PLANNING COMMISSION TO VOTE ON LAND-USE ISSUE

AFFECTING 200,000 ACRES OF BACKCOUNTRY FARMLAND

San Diego – The most important land-use decision affecting the backcountry of San Diego since the Forest Conservation Initiative will go before the Planning Commission tomorrow, March 3, 2000 at a hearing to begin at 9 am. This vote will determine the fate of over 200,000 acres of Agricultural Preserve (Ag 20) open space and farmlands. It will also determine whether urban sprawl will engulf the backcountry with I-15 traffic all the way to Julian, or whether we will preserve our valuable and irreplaceable Agricultural Preserve (Ag 20) lands.

The County’s project, GPA 96-03, is the response to SOFAR’s lawsuit against the County for not adopting an Agriculture Element and not properly zoning our Agricultural Preserve lands. The Superior Court ruled that the General Plan was missing a mandatory Agriculture Element and the urban zoning of 8-acre minimum lot size set by the County for Agricultural Preserve (Ag 20) lands was illegal. All subdivision activity and issuance of building permits was stopped.

GPA 96-03, as proposed by the County, allows 10-acre minimum lot sizes for Ag 20 lands west of the CWA infrastructure line. Ag 20 lands east of the CWA infrastructure line would have a 40-acre minimum lot size or a 10-acre minimum lot size if it can be demonstrated that there is evidence of adequate soils, topography, and water availability to support the proposed agriculture.

The County’s own Environmental Impact Report (EIR) for GPA 96-03 assumes complete clearing of the 200,000 acres of Agricultural Preserve (Ag 20) farmlands. The EIR finds significant impacts to Traffic, Water Resources, Visual Quality/Aesthetics, Cultural/Paleontological, Geological Issues, Noise, and Air Quality. This assumption of complete clearing of the backcountry is not done to facilitate agriculture; this is a mascarade for the complete development of San Diego’s backcountry. The lot size minimums proposed by the County will not support agriculture. Instead, these lot size minimums invite ranchette development.

SOFAR’s Alternative for GPA 96-03 is:
The County of San Diego adopt an Agriculture Element to our General Plan to cover several hundred thousand acres of irrigated cropland west of the County Water Authority (CWA) infrastructure line.

Adopt lot size minimums for Agriculture Preserve (Ag 20) farmlands and open space of 80 acres east of the CWA infrastructure line, 40 acres west of the CWA line, and 10 acres for prime farmland west of the CWA line.

This alternative meets the requirements of the Williamson Act, for which these lands are set aside, and the need to preserve our backcountry Agricultural Preserve (Ag 20) open space and farmlands. These Agricultural Preserve lands are vital to our quality of life and the future of San Diego County. The vote at tomorrow’s Planning Commission hearing will determine what type of backcountry we will leave to our children and future generations of San Diegans.

The Planning Commission hearing will take place tomorrow, March 3, 2000 at 9 am in the Planning Commission Hearing Room at the Department of Planning & Land Use at 5201 Ruffin Road, San Diego.
