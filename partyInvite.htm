Invitation to celebrate

SAVE THE FOREST

The Third CNFF Celebration
Honoring Theodore Roosevelt’s 142nd Birthday
And His Enduring Vision for Our Forest

On October 27, 1858, one of America’s greatest environmentalists was born:

President Theodore Roosevelt.
His leadership and vision resulted in a stunning gift to future generations:
The Cleveland National Forest.

By setting aside 2,000.000 acres of magnificent wilderness from Mt. San Jacinto to the Mexican border, the beauty of open space, trees, surrounding vegetation and all the wildlife it sustains was to be ours forever. Unfortunately, we have this irreplaceable heritage to decline to 650,000 acres.

The Cleveland National Forest Foundation was created for the sole purpose of protecting what remains of his gift. We ask that you join in this momentous endeavor.

Celebrate with us the Foundation’s success in acquiring Ellis Ranch and donating this priceless entrance to the Cleveland National Forest and gateway to Cuyamuca Rancho State Park to the United States Forest Service. And join with us for the launching of CNFF’s Conservation Easement Program to protect wildlands and watershed in the Forest.

Twilight on the Hacienda
October 15, 2000
2 – 7 p.m.

Rancho Samataguma
Guatay, California

Spend the afternoon at San Diego’s spectacular Rancho Samataguma. This gorgeous ranch is nestled in its own valley and is surrounded by the mountains of the Cleveland National Forest. Rancho Samataguma is both a working cattle ranch and delightful home to the numerous other denizens of the ranch. Feel free to stroll the exquisite grounds of the gracious Old California Spanish-style “ranch house”.

Local musicians, dancers, performers, and artisans will provide a variety of entertainment.

Gourmet cuisine and drink will be provided by the Picnic People and is generously underwritten by a grant from Hospitality Inc.
