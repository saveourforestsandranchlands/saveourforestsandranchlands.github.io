The Watchdog Newsletter
16 October 2000

ROUTING THE ENEMY!

The lawyer for the Lakeside Ralph’s project has just sent a letter recommending that the project applicants conduct a full Environmental Impact Report (EIR) for the project. Admittedly, this project will greatly impact the traffic and run-off of the area. This project is more suitable for an urban area rather than rural east Lakeside. The letter was received by the Chairman of the Lakeside Planning Group.
This is encouraging news to SOFAR and our Supporters that wish to hold the line of development at the urban/rural edge. We have held the line against urban sprawl at Alpine, with our success in requiring the Alpine Albertsons proponents to complete an EIR, and now at Lakeside. These areas should not be allowed the intensive, urban development that is more appropriate to El Cajon and La Mesa where urban services currently exist. Another front is Ramona, where the rural/urban edge is at Poway. Approving urban-style projects in Alpine, Lakeside, and Ramona is leap-frog development that will allow urban sprawl to spread into San Diego County’s backcountry.

TWILIGHT AT THE HACIENDA

The Cleveland National Forest Foundation would like to thank all those who attended the benefit party held at Rancho Samataguma yesterday. They would also like to thank the Browns for graciously allowing them to use the ranch for the party.
The event was well-attended; the ranch was beautiful; the weather splendid; Michael Soule’s talk was informative and inspiring; the food was delicious; the company was outstanding; and the day was enjoyed by all.
Thank you from CNFF!
